# Retirement Planning and Exact Age Calculations

When planning for retirement, "around 65" isn't an acceptable metric. Financial institutions, government agencies, and tax codes rely on your exact age—down to the month and day—to determine your eligibility for benefits and penalty-free withdrawals.

## The Magic Numbers of Retirement

In the United States, several exact ages act as critical financial triggers:
- **Age 59½:** The age you can generally begin withdrawing from IRAs and 401(k)s without facing a 10% early withdrawal penalty.
- **Age 62:** The earliest age you can claim Social Security retirement benefits, though benefits will be permanently reduced.
- **Age 67 (Full Retirement Age):** Depending on your birth year, 67 is the age you are entitled to 100% of your Social Security benefits.
- **Age 73:** The age at which Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) must begin from traditional retirement accounts (as of recent tax law changes).

## Calculating Your Half-Birthdays

Because the IRS heavily utilizes "half-birthdays" (e.g., 59½), precise date calculation is vital. A half-birthday is exactly six calendar months after your actual birthday.

## Precision Matters

Mistakes in retirement calculations can be costly. Withdrawing funds from a 401(k) just one day before you turn 59½ can trigger severe tax penalties.
